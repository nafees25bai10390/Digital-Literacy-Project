Title: Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. These attacks are usually carried out through emails, messages, or fake websites that look legitimate.

In a typical scenario, a college student receives an email that appears to be from their bank. The email states that there is an urgent issue with their account and asks them to click on a link to verify their details. The message creates a sense of urgency, warning that the account will be blocked if immediate action is not taken. Trusting the message, the student clicks on the link, which redirects them to a fake website designed to look exactly like the official bank website.

The student then enters their login credentials, including their username, password, and OTP. Once this information is submitted, it is captured by the attacker. Within minutes, the attacker gains access to the student’s bank account and performs unauthorized transactions, leading to financial loss.

Phishing attacks often target students, employees, and elderly individuals because they may not always recognize fake messages or websites. These attacks are dangerous because they exploit human emotions like fear and urgency rather than technical vulnerabilities.

The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal information. In some cases, victims may also face long-term issues such as compromised accounts and loss of trust in digital platforms.

This case study highlights the importance of awareness and caution while interacting with emails and online links. Users should always verify the authenticity of messages, avoid clicking on suspicious links, and never share sensitive information online without proper verification.
